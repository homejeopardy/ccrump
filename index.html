Catherine Crump

Clinical Professor of Law
Director, Samuelson Law, Technology & Public Policy Clinic
UC Berkeley School of Law

Catherine Crump teaches, writes, and litigates on the intersection of technology and civil liberties. At Berkeley Law she directs the Samuelson Clinic, where students work on real-world technology law and policy projects that advance the public interest.

Projects

  • Surveillance Oversight: Supporting community groups and policymakers in crafting oversight frameworks for police surveillance technologies.
  • Digital Rights Advocacy: Developing strategies to protect privacy, free speech, and democratic values in the digital age.
  • Public Records & Transparency: Litigating and advocating for access to government information about technology use.
