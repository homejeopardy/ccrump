About

Catherine Crump is a Clinical Professor of Law at UC Berkeley School of Law, where she directs the Samuelson Law, Technology & Public Policy Clinic. Her work focuses on the intersection of civil liberties and government use of new technologies, particularly in the areas of surveillance, policing, and privacy.

Projects

  • Government Transparency and Surveillance — Advocating for greater accountability in how state and local governments use surveillance technologies.
  • Automated Decision-Making in the Public Sector — Examining how AI and algorithms affect fairness, transparency, and due process in government decision-making.
  • Technology and Civil Liberties — Researching how new digital tools impact privacy, free speech, and democratic accountability.
