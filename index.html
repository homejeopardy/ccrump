About

Catherine Crump is a Clinical Professor of Law and the Director of the Samuelson Law, Technology & Public Policy Clinic at UC Berkeley School of Law. Her teaching and advocacy focus on the intersection of law, technology, and civil liberties, with an emphasis on government surveillance, data privacy, and the social implications of emerging technologies.

Research & Teaching

Professor Crump’s research and clinical projects explore how legal frameworks adapt to rapid technological change. She works closely with students to produce scholarship and advocacy that addresses pressing issues at the intersection of technology and the public interest.

  • Government surveillance and the Fourth Amendment
  • Data privacy and consumer protection
  • Algorithmic accountability and automated decision-making
  • Transparency in law enforcement technologies

Selected Projects

Accountability for Police Surveillance Technologies

A multi-year initiative examining how law enforcement agencies deploy new surveillance tools such as drones, automated license plate readers, and predictive policing software. The project advocates for transparency, oversight, and compliance with constitutional safeguards.

Data Privacy in the Digital Economy

Clinical work supporting legislative and regulatory proposals designed to strengthen consumer privacy protections in the United States, particularly with respect to companies’ collection and use of personal data.

Civil Liberties and Emerging Technologies

Research and advocacy focused on the civil liberties implications of technologies such as facial recognition, artificial intelligence, and biometric identification systems, with an emphasis on preventing discriminatory impacts and promoting democratic oversight.
